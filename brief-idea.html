02 How It Works
LLM agents read the reports.
Weather data enters the graph.

No ML models trained on 3 depeg events. An agentic LLM pipeline extracts counterparty data from attestation PDFs, builds a geographic knowledge graph, and injects weather signals as stress inputs on specific nodes.

Attestation Extraction

LLM reads Circle, Tether, Paxos PDFs. Extracts banks, percentages, asset classes, maturity profiles, jurisdictions.

Circle · Tether · Paxos reports

Geographic Knowledge Graph

Stablecoin → Bank → City/State → Asset Class. Geographic anchoring makes weather data actionable.

Extracted data · FDIC · geocoding

Weather Signal Injection

NOAA forecasts and NHC tracks enter the graph as stress modifiers on geographic nodes. A storm in Miami stresses Miami bank nodes.

NOAA · NHC · OpenMeteo · FEMA

Counterparty Monitoring

Named banks tracked against news, FDIC watch lists, CDS spreads. Weather stress compounds with financial stress on the same nodes.

News APIs · FDIC · financial data

Deterministic Risk Scoring

Weighted composite across six dimensions. Rules handle quantifiable signals, LLM reasons over qualitative ones. Every score traces to named entities.

Rule engine · LLM reasoning

LLM Risk Narratives

Full causal chain in plain English. Not "72/100" but "12% of reserves at First Regional FL, facing $4B in hurricane claims exposure."

Claude API · chain tracing

07 The Killer Demo
Three pre-loaded scenarios.
All guaranteed to work.

Demo flow: 90 seconds that win the hackathon

1

Show the dashboard. Six stablecoins scored across six dimensions. Traffic lights mostly green. Map shows custodian bank locations from attestation reports. "We built this by having an LLM read every major attestation report and map the counterparties geographically."

2

Drop a hurricane on the map. Select "Category 4, SE Florida." The storm cone appears. Banks in the impact zone highlight. Scores recalculate as stress propagates through the graph. Two stablecoins shift green to yellow.

3

Click into the explanation. The LLM generates: "USDC counterparty risk elevated. 12% of reserves held at institutions in SE Florida. Projected $4.2B insurance liability in Miami-Dade increases deposit stress on regional custodians."

4

Switch to the SVB backtest. "Let's rewind to March 2023." Timeline shows Katabatic's score turning yellow 48 hours before the depeg. "Counterparty concentration: 8.3% of reserves at SVB, showing elevated deposit outflow signals."

5

Close the pitch. "Monthly audits told you USDC was fine. Katabatic would have told you to move your money. The risk isn't on the blockchain. It's in the weather, the banks, the geography."
